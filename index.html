Kevin Cong

I am currently an undergraduate at Harvard University studying math and statistics with a masters in statistics. I am broadly interested in statistical theory, machine learning theory, and their applications to modern generative models. During my time at Harvard, I have worked on research in math (combinatorics) at the Duluth REU and ML theory (optimizing sampling schedules for masked diffusion) with Professor Sitan Chen. Starting August 2026, I will be a quantitative trader at Jane Street Capital.

Outside of my research and career work, I am always looking for new things to learn. Currently, my main endeavor in this regard is reading old and new material in modern machine learning, in particular in deep learning, RL/reasoning, and diffusion. Outside of academics, I mostly love to meet and talk to friends about interesting ideas. I am also an avid fan of classical music & literature, poker, piano, philosophy, and board games.
